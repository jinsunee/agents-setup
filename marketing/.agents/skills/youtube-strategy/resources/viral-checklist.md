This is Codie Sanchez’s checklist for content that goes viral. It’s not about checking every box — but the more you hit, the higher your odds of going viral. 

Use it as a gut check before you hit publish, or as a roadmap while you build. Start testing, tweaking, and tracking what works.

## 1. Audience Pain Point

Specific pain desire, or challenge that resonates with audience.

*Example: How do you make $1M when you have $0?*

## 2. Promise of Transformation

Promise tangible outcome that addressed audience’s pain.

*Example: This strategy turned my $500 side hustle into a 7-figure business.*

## 3. Evoke Emotion

Verbal or non-verbal language to captivate audience attention.

*Example: You don’t need another course. You need to start.*

## 4. Contrarian Thinking

Idea that challenges conventional thinking, out of the norm.

*Example: College is a scam. Here’s what I did instead.*

## 5. Specificity & Numbers

Stats, details, or graphs to make hook more actionable.

*Example: He made $18,472.12 in one week by…*

## 6. Urgency or Timeliness

Highlight timeliness to take immediate action.

*Example:* T*his opportunity won’t exist in 2026.*

## 7. Experience & Familiarity

Personal story to establish credibility and connection.

*Example: I was $40K in debt with no plan. Here’s how I built a 6-figure business.*

## 8. Motivate

Remind people they have potential to achieve the promised transformation. 

*Example: Someone with half your talent is already doing it.*

## 9. Concise

No filler words, convey message efficiently without unnecessary stuff.

*Example: Move fast, break things. Investing is math, not feelings.*

## 10. Enhancer Words

Use words that evoke emotion like secret, money, mistake.

*Example: My biggest money mistake (and how I fixed it).*

Use famous words/people to spark curiosity like Elon Musk, Taylor Swift, Apple.
